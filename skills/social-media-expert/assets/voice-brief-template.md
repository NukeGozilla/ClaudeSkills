# Voice Brief Template

Paste this into the chat when you need to capture a user's voice for the first time, or when you're working with a new voice for a specific project. The user only has to answer what's relevant — skip questions that don't apply.

---

## Quick voice brief

**1. Who is the speaker?**
- Name / role / company (or "personal brand of [name]"):
- One-sentence description a stranger would understand:

**2. Who is the intended audience?**
- Who is this content *really* for? (Be specific — "Series A B2B founders who just hired their first marketer," not "business owners")
- What problem are they trying to solve?
- What's the one thing you want them to think/feel/do after reading your posts?

**3. Voice — the shape of how you sound**
- 3 words that describe your ideal voice (e.g., "direct, warm, slightly irreverent"):
- 3 words that describe voices you want to AVOID sounding like (e.g., "corporate, motivational, buzzwordy"):
- Formality: [casual / professional-casual / professional / formal]
- First person or third? ("I" / "we" / brand voice)

**4. Samples (most useful part — please paste 2-3 of each if possible)**
- 2-3 posts you've written that you liked (yours):
- 2-3 posts by others whose voice you'd like yours to resemble (share the text or links):
- 1-2 posts that capture what you DON'T want to sound like:

**5. Must-haves and must-avoids**
- Phrases/words you always use:
- Phrases/words you never want to see in your content:
- Topics that are in-bounds:
- Topics that are off-bounds:

**6. Practical constraints**
- Primary platform(s):
- Posting cadence you'll realistically hit:
- Goal (reach / engagement / leads / authority / community):

---

Once you have the user's answers, summarize back a 3-4 line "voice card" and confirm:

> "Voice card — [Name]:
> Speaker: [role, 1 line]
> Audience: [1 line]
> Tone: [3 words]
> Signature moves: [2-3 distinctive things they do]
> Avoid: [2-3 things they never do]"

This voice card is what you'll keep in mind when drafting. If the user corrects part of it, update it before drafting anything new.
