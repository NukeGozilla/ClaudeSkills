# Platform Playbooks

Each platform rewards different things. Read the section for the platform you're working on before drafting — the norms, character limits, and algorithmic preferences are specific enough that generic advice will produce mediocre output.

## 1. LinkedIn

**What it's for:** Professional credibility, B2B reach, recruiting, thought leadership, deal flow. Best platform for reaching senior decision-makers and selling to businesses.

**Character limits:**
- Post: 3,000 chars (but "see more" truncates around line 3; the first 3 lines matter most)
- Comments: 1,250 chars
- Article (long-form): 125,000 chars
- Headline: 220 chars

**What performs (late 2025):**
- **Text posts with a strong 3-line hook.** The "dwell time" algorithm rewards posts where people pause to expand and read.
- **LinkedIn-native documents (carousels built in Canva/SlideShare exported as PDF).** 5-10 slides, high text-to-visual ratio, each slide is a complete micro-idea. Still one of the highest-reaching formats.
- **Polls** for engagement but usually low-quality interactions; good for surfacing questions, weak for real conversation.
- **Video natively uploaded** (not YouTube links) with captions baked in. 60-90 seconds is the sweet spot.
- **Storytelling + specific number** combo: "We lost our biggest customer last quarter. Here's the $2.1M mistake I made" outperforms abstract takes.

**What doesn't work:**
- External links in the post body (algorithmically suppressed). Put the link in the first comment and reference "link in comments" in the post — but only when the link genuinely adds value.
- Generic motivational content ("Grateful for the journey…").
- Humblebrags that read as brags.
- Recycled HBR takeaways dressed up as original insight.
- Over-tagging people who aren't relevant.

**Hook patterns that still work:**
- **Counterintuitive claim:** "We stopped doing demos. Sales went up 40%."
- **Specific failure:** "I fired myself from my own company last year. Best decision I made."
- **Numbered list preview:** "7 things I'd do differently if I started my company again (#3 surprised me)"
- **Dialogue/scene:** `My investor: "You're going to lose this deal." / Me: "I know." / Here's why I did it anyway.`

**Hashtags:** 3-5 max. Mix one broad (#Leadership) with 2-3 niche (#B2BSaaS, #FounderLife). More than 5 looks spammy.

**Posting cadence:** 2-5x/week is the sweet spot for most creators. Daily can work for full-time LinkedIn creators but is overkill for most professionals. Tuesday-Thursday mornings (user's timezone) tend to perform best.

**Comment strategy:** Replying to comments in the first 60 minutes of a post meaningfully extends reach. Treat the comments as the second act of the post, not an afterthought.

## 2. X (Twitter)

**What it's for:** Real-time commentary, community-building, industry conversations, distribution to other tech/media people, building in public. Fast, punchy, conversational.

**Character limits:**
- Post: 280 chars free / up to 25,000 for X Premium subscribers
- Thread: Technically unlimited; practically 5-15 posts

**What performs (late 2025):**
- **Short standalone posts with a sharp take.** "Everyone is missing the point about X" style. Replies are where deals actually happen.
- **Threads (5-12 posts)** for tutorials, narratives, or case studies. Strong hook in tweet 1, one idea per tweet, tweet 12 (or wherever it ends) has the CTA.
- **Replies to bigger accounts** — often higher ROI than original posts for newer accounts trying to build presence.
- **Screenshots** of longer thoughts (the "screenshot essay") can work for users with large enough followings.
- **Community notes and corrections** that add real value.

**What doesn't work:**
- Long preamble. Every word costs.
- Hashtags in the post body (they lower reach).
- Link-heavy posts (algorithm suppresses). Put links in reply ("link below ↓").
- Engagement bait ("RT if you agree!").

**Hook patterns:**
- **Sharp declarative:** "Most 'personal brand' advice is LARPing as an entrepreneur."
- **Mini-scene:** "My co-founder just sent me a one-line Slack that killed our biggest feature."
- **List preview:** "5 tactics that 3x'd our sign-ups. Most took under an hour."
- **Contrarian question:** "Why is nobody talking about this obvious problem with RAG?"

**Thread structure:**
1. Tweet 1: Hook + promise (what will they learn?)
2. Tweets 2-N-1: One idea each, short, punchy, often with a line break between sentences for rhythm
3. Final tweet: CTA — follow for more, retweet the first tweet, or link to deeper resource

**Hashtags:** Essentially dead on X for discovery. Use 0-1, only if the hashtag is an active community tag (like #buildinpublic).

**Posting cadence:** Heavy X users post 3-10x/day. For most people, 1-3 thoughtful posts + active replies is plenty.

**Reply game:** Consistently underrated. The path to 10k followers on X is usually "reply thoughtfully to 5 accounts just above your level, every day, for 6 months."

## 3. Instagram

**What it's for:** Visual brand-building, creator-driven growth, lifestyle/consumer/product discovery. Image and video-first.

**Character limits:**
- Caption: 2,200 chars
- Bio: 150 chars
- Comments: 2,200 chars

**Formats and what performs (late 2025):**
- **Reels** (short video, 15-90s) — the highest-reach format. Hook in first 1.5 seconds, trending audio often helps but not required.
- **Carousels** (up to 10 slides) — second-highest reach; high dwell time because people swipe. Educational or before/after carousels perform best.
- **Single photos** — lowest reach unless you have an engaged following. Best used as part of a consistent aesthetic.
- **Stories** — not for reach, for relationship depth with existing followers. Polls, Q&A stickers, behind-the-scenes.
- **Collabs (dual-posting)** — huge for growth when done with creators in adjacent niches.

**Caption strategy:**
- First line is the hook (same principle as LinkedIn — only ~125 chars visible before "more").
- Captions can be long (500-1,500 chars works well for educational accounts) or tight and witty (for lifestyle/creator accounts).
- End with a question OR a clear CTA ("save this if you'll forget / share with a friend who needs to hear this") — but only if genuine.

**Hashtags:**
- 3-8 relevant tags. Mix 1-2 big (#productivity, 10M+ posts), 3-4 mid (#productivitytips, 100k-1M), 2-3 niche (#productivityforfounders, <100k).
- Put them at the end of the caption or in the first comment — both work; no meaningful difference anymore.
- Avoid banned/shadowbanned hashtags (do a quick check — search the tag on IG; if "Recent" isn't showing, it's likely flagged).

**Reels hooks:**
- Visual pattern interrupt in the first frame (unexpected motion, text overlay, facial expression).
- Text-on-screen hook that states the payoff: "3 things I'd never do as a small business owner."
- Ask-and-answer: "Why is my IG not growing? It's usually one of these 3 things."

**Posting cadence:** 3-5 Reels/week + daily Stories is a sustainable high-growth cadence. Posting more than once a day can cannibalize reach.

## 4. TikTok

**What it's for:** Short-form video reach at scale. Fastest-growing audience discovery platform. Works for both personal creators and brands that can show personality.

**Character limits:**
- Caption: 2,200 chars (but anything over ~100 hurts you — viewers skip)
- Video: 3 seconds to 10 minutes; sweet spot is 21-34 seconds for most content

**What performs (late 2025):**
- **Strong visual and verbal hook in the first 1-2 seconds.** TikTok's algorithm decides fast. Pattern breaks, direct-to-camera questions, surprising visuals, or a bold on-screen text all work.
- **Native-feeling content** — polished, over-produced videos often underperform raw, personality-led ones.
- **Hooks with a pause/pattern break at ~3 seconds** to capture re-watches (rewatch rate is a massive ranking factor).
- **On-screen text** reinforcing the audio — most people watch with sound but on-screen text helps retention.
- **Carousels (photo mode)** are having a resurgence for educational content.
- **Duets and Stitches** — leveraging existing viral content.

**Content patterns:**
- **POV / story time** — "Story time: the client who ghosted me after approving the contract."
- **Listicle with payoff at end** — "3 things I'd never say in a job interview… #3 nobody talks about."
- **Before/after / reveal** — strong visual promise, deliver the reveal in the final 3 seconds.
- **Tutorial** — teach something in 30 seconds with on-screen steps.

**Caption:**
- 1-2 sentences max. The caption is not where the content lives — the video is.
- 1-3 hashtags (mix of niche and broad). Don't stuff.
- Avoid "link in bio" as the whole CTA; integrate it naturally.

**Hashtags:** 3-5 tags. One very broad (#fyp rarely helps, skip it), 2-3 niche-relevant, 1-2 trend-specific if riding a wave.

**Posting cadence:** Daily if you're growing; 3-4x/week as a minimum viable for an active account. TikTok rewards volume more than the other platforms.

**Sounds:** Using a trending sound can 3-5x reach if it fits organically. Forcing a trend to a misaligned message is worse than no trend.

## 5. YouTube

**What it's for:** Depth, durability (videos get found and watched for years), SEO, monetization, community trust. Best platform for converting viewers into customers/subscribers because attention is high-quality.

**Two distinct formats:**

### 5a. YouTube Shorts (≤60 seconds)

- Functionally similar to TikTok/Reels. Vertical, sound-on, snappy hooks.
- Strong hook in the first 1 second. Payoff promise, then deliver.
- Thumbnails matter less than title + first-second visual.
- Captions/on-screen text strongly recommended.

### 5b. Long-form YouTube (8-20 min sweet spot for most channels)

**What matters most (in order):**
1. **Thumbnail + title combo** — ~80% of whether someone clicks. Clear, high-contrast, face or object large, 3-5 words of text max. Title should promise a specific outcome or curiosity gap without being clickbait.
2. **Hook (first 15 seconds)** — restate why the viewer should stay. No logo animations, no "hey guys welcome back."
3. **Retention graph** — if the algorithm sees people drop off at minute 3, it won't promote further. Cold open, re-hook around the low points, deliver on the promise.
4. **CTAs that serve the viewer** — subscribe prompts mid-video (after delivering value) outperform end-prompts; lower third reminders are less disruptive than mid-roll speeches.

**Description:** First 150 chars get shown in search. Pack the keyword and outcome there. Include timestamps for videos >5 min.

**Posting cadence:** 1/week is the industry standard. Quality > quantity. Consistent schedule > irregular bursts.

## 6. Facebook

**What it's for:** Still meaningful for community (Facebook Groups), local businesses, older demographics (35-65+), event promotion, and ad targeting. Organic reach for Pages is low — Groups are where the energy is.

**Character limits:**
- Post: 63,206 chars (but posts over 80 words drop in reach sharply)
- Comments: 8,000 chars

**What performs:**
- **Short, emotional posts** (under 80 words) on Pages.
- **Longer, narrative posts in Groups.** Group content gets much better reach than Page content.
- **Native video** (1-3 min) with captions.
- **Live video** — still gets prioritized; great for Q&As, launches, community events.
- **Event posts** for local businesses/communities.

**What doesn't work:**
- Links to external sites (heavy algorithmic suppression on Pages).
- Bare image posts without context.
- Long, rambling posts with no hook.

**Hashtags:** Use 1-3, but they barely help. Mostly cosmetic.

**Posting cadence:** 3-5x/week on Pages; Groups should be wherever the community naturally engages.

## 7. Threads

**What it's for:** Casual, text-first conversation. Feels like early X. Good for creators with IG followings to port over; less essential for pure B2B.

**Character limits:**
- Post: 500 chars
- Can attach up to 10 images/videos

**What performs:**
- **Casual, in-the-moment takes.** Less polished than LinkedIn or long Instagram captions.
- **Reply culture is strong** — the conversation IS the content.
- **Text-heavy + occasional image.**
- **Connected with IG audience** — porting IG followers who then engage on Threads is the main growth path.

**Hashtags/mentions:** One hashtag per post max, and mostly unnecessary.

**Posting cadence:** 1-5x/day for engaged creators; whenever for everyone else. Threads punishes silence less than X does.

## 8. Cross-platform adaptation notes

When the user asks you to "post this everywhere," push back and adapt. Here's the short translation guide:

| If the original is for… | Adapt to LinkedIn by… | Adapt to X by… | Adapt to IG/Reels by… | Adapt to TikTok by… |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Blog post** | Pulling one insight + a specific number, ~200 words | 5-8 tweet thread, one idea per tweet | Carousel: 1 insight per slide, 6-8 slides | Video: top insight as a hook, 30s explainer |
| **LinkedIn post** | *(source)* | Cut 70%, keep the sharpest take | Rewrite as carousel with visuals | Turn into a direct-to-camera hook + payoff |
| **Tweet** | Expand the context and narrative | *(source)* | Pair with an image or turn into a Reel | Record it as a 15s direct-to-camera |
| **Video script** | Pull the 3 best quotes; write a narrative post | Thread with one quote per tweet | The video itself (or an edit) | The video itself (vertical format) |

The core rule: **preserve the insight, not the wording.** Same idea, platform-native expression.
